CLEAR-AMR looks for campus places where waste, water, food, animals, medicines, or poor hygiene may create AMR-relevant risks. Students document a possible risk route — not prove AMR is present.

Project aim

Understand what the site visits are for before going out.

Students do not prove that AMR is present. Students document a possible risk route and what may reduce it.

Key words

Use these four concepts during every site visit.

SourceWhere the concern starts.
Way it movesHands, flies, water, shoes, animals, surfaces.
Who it reachesPeople, animals, food, water, soil, surfaces.
ControlWhat could reduce the route.
Do not write that AMR is present unless there is laboratory evidence.

Read the site note and build a risk route sentence.

Practice: evidence

Separate what was seen, heard, assumed, and unknown.

A student says leftover antibiotics are sometimes thrown into a hostel bin. No medicines are seen during the visit.

Questions and conduct

Ask only what helps the site decision. Keep it short and respectful.

Good questions are short, respectful, and practical. Do not ask every question on the list.

Site visit tool

Eight steps — use the Visit tab on this app.

1 · StartSite ID, visit type, date, students, person spoken to.
2 · LookTick visible concerns. Write one observation.
3 · AskA few short questions if someone is available.
4 · Risk routeBuild the source → movement → exposure sentence.
5 · EvidencePhotos and a short note only.
6 · ClassifyRoute type, contact, feasibility, confidence.
7 · Check thinkingSeen / heard / assumed / uncertain.
8 · ExportRecommendation, reason, export JSON + PDF.
